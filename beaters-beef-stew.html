Beaters Beef Stew

So this Stew is something I cooked for many years and was regularly enjoyed by the local shoot Beaters all through the shooting season. Beaters are the people country folk are used to hearing drive game birds. It is often a cold and tiring job so you need a substaincial hot lunch. But as the lunch was free to arround 20 to 30 people it had to be low in cost so it's perfect in the current crisis. Packed with vegetables the only change as this is for a dinner and I am only feeding us, I have used ale but you can simply use water it will still be wonderful. Keep your slices and dices fairly large as we are going to cook this for 4 hrs low and slow. Lentils are added purerly to thicken the stew and will disapear completely. You can of course use a slow cooker for 8 hours or Pressure cooker for 45 minutes.

  • 1 tsp sea salt crushed
  • 1 tsp black pepper crushed
  • 1 Beef OXO cube crumbled
  • 1 vegetable OXO stock cube crumbled
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 350g Whole Braising Steak diced 2cm cubes
  • 1 Tbls plain flour
  • 1 tsp dyjon mustard
  • 3 Tbls vegetable oil
  • 2 large bay leaves
  • 140g shallots peeled & whole
  • 140g carrots sliced
  • 2 medium leeks sliced
  • 1 medium onion finely diced
  • 130g mushrooms quatered
  • 550g tin of new potatoes sliced
  • 110g Moong Dhal lentils
  • 500 ml bottle of Ale (I used Bishops Finger)
  • 300ml water
  • A good dash of Hendersons Relish or Worschester sauce

method

I buy my Braising beef as whole steaks as you have full control over the dice. If you do not have moong dhal please use yellow or red lentils. Take time to slice and dice everything in advance as evenly as you can. I have used tinned new potatoes as they hold there shape well and will not dissolve into the stew, but feel free to use fresh if you choose. Once everything is prepared chopped and weighed this if simple to fry off combine cover and you will be free for 4 hours to do as you wish. I of course wrote up this recipe to share with you all, as Nutty often does once that job is done, I may simply have a nice cup of tea ;-).

  1. Crush your 1 tsp salt and 1 tsp black pepper in a pestle & mortar add your Beef & vegetable OXO stock cubes crumbled add Thyme and set aside
  2. Pre-heat your large pan with 3 Tbls of oil and two bay leaves on a high setting
  3. Place your diced beef into 1 Tbls of plain flour and coat well add in 1 Tsp of dyjon mustard mix through.
  4. Add half the beef stir fry add the other half and allow to brown 10 minutes stir ocasionally.
  5. Add in your shallots stir fry through, now add the sliced carrots combine.
  6. Add the sliced leeks stir fry through, now add the finely diced onion combine.
  7. Push everything to one side of the pan add your quartered mushrooms allow to fry slightly.
  8. Combine everything in the pan add new potaoes then 110g Moong Dhal lentils.

*See below if not cooking on the hob.

  1. Add in all your seasoning & herbs now add your 550ml Ale & 300ml water stir to combine.
  2. Cover with a lid allow to come to just a boil then turn the ring to very low. I am using a large ring so it was set to level 2 heat for 4 hours on a timer.

*Slow cooker

Method once all is browned & ready set into slow cooker add liquids set onto low for 8 hours.

*Pressure cooker

Method once all is browned & ready set into presure cooker add liquids & add liquids set onto stew for 45 minutes.
