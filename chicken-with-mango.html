Chicken with Mango

Chicken with Mango Do not dismiss fruit from curry's it is a staple all over the world. Adding any fruits including mango will tenderise your chicken within minutes. The little addition of another layer of mango cubes, freshly chopped tomatoes and green chillies are delightful. The hardest bit of this curry is pealing and slicing the mango. Slippery little buggers! Do it over a deep dish to reserve all those juices to tenderise your meat. This is in no way a sweet or indeed hot dish, the balance of spices and fresh juicy sweetness are a perfect mathc for each other.

(serves 4)

for the spice paste / marinade

  • 1 fresh mango
  • 1/2 tsp onion seeds
  • 2 tsp garlic pulp
  • 2 tsp ginger pulp
  • 1 tsp hot chilli powder
  • 2 tsp kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 450g diced chicken thighs

to cook with

  • 2 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 medium onions thickly sliced
  • 8 to 10 Curry leaves
  • 300ml water
  • 2 large tomatoes sliced
  • 2 fresh green chillis sliced
  • 2 Tbls fresh coriander

method

Now the mango needs peeling throughly, the skins are tough and will ruin your quick cook curry instantly! So peel it over a dish so as not to lose the juices. Look for signs of the stone and cut arround the edge. Then slip your knife into the edge. Now, I tend to use my fingers to try and slip the stone out. Then once you have the stone, take a small knife and skim off any remaining flesh. First time — knightmare! But, once you get to know this fruit you will learn to love it :-)

  1. So the mango challenge is done, now split it into two, as half is for the marriande & the rest will be added right at the end of the curry
  2. Now lets work on the spices, it's easy! Grind any whole spices you have like corriander with sea salt, it helps break the seeds down
  3. Combine with the ground spices, add garlic paste & ginger
  4. Dice your chicken, combine the mango, spice mix & chicken
  5. Set this aside into the fridge to marinate — just while you do the dreaded browning onions "Shakes head"
  6. Heat a heavy based pan, add the oil, once hot reduce the heat add your onions
  7. And yes wait wait, wait.. wait! When brownish add the curry leaves & stir fry gently
  8. Now add a tablespoon or two of your chicken mixture, gradually! Try not to rush this stage please*

*If you chuck it all in at once it will boil. We want it to fry each side of the chicken and this requires some patience, I fry mine in about 50g batches

  1. Once each batch looks white & it all looks, well stir fried through the mango & onions, add the next batch
  2. Now we add water just, to cover the chicken, I say 300ml but I can not see your pan, just cover the meat
  3. Now let it cook for 10 to 15 minutes with out a lid! We want it to dry out so when we add our next stage it's a thick sauce
  4. Now add your tomatoes, reserved mango cubes, green chilli slices & fresh or frozen corriander
  5. Cover for 5 minutes to allow the dish to reheat & serve
