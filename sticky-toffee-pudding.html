Sticky Toffee Pudding and Caramel Sauce

Now this is a truely decadant dessert. Rich, moist and the dark brown sugar creates a caramel note that is to die for. Be sure you have some friends to help you devour it as eating a whole one may be not advisable :-). It can just be eaten like cake but here i am adding a caramel sauce made with light brown sugar. It is fast to put together so lets do it.

Click the Method tab below to see a step-by-step guide on how to make Sticky Toffee Pudding and Caramel Sauce along with some pictures as a guide.

ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

serves 8

Ingredients for the Pudding

  • 75g soft butter
  • 175g dark brown demerara sugar
  • 200g self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 Tbsp golden syrup
  • 2 Tbsp black treacle
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 200g pitted dried dates
  • 1 Tbsp bicarbonate of soda

Ingredients for the Caramel Sauce

  • 100g light brown sugar
  • 100g butter
  • 200ml double cream

method

Like all cakes it is best to get your self organised!

Our first job is to line our tin with butter and flour and get the oven pre heated to 200c. Then it's a simple cake batter but this one requires self raising flour & bicarb so once the raising agents are added the cake needs to be got rapidly into the oven.

One great thing about this dessert is it can be reheated and even frozen.

So with the oven on, tin buttered and floured...

  1. Get a small sauce pan, stick blender and a mixer.
  2. Weigh out all your ingredients and place the butter and sugar in the mixer then blend until creamy.
  1. when creamed add the following to the mixer:
    • 2 eggs
    • 1 Tbsp golden syrup
    • 2 Tbsp black treacle
    • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  1. Add your flour but dont blend yet!

Now to get our dates ready.../p>

  1. Place dates and 300ml water into the sauce pan and heat to a simmer.
  1. Now blend the dates with a stick blender.
  1. Add the bicarb to the hot date mixture.
  2. Tip into the mixer with the other ingredients & mix till combined. Cake batter is now done!
  3. Immediately! Transfer to your prepared cake tin and set in the oven for 40 to 45 minutes.

In the mean time...

  1. Melt the butter, add the sugar & mix together on the hob
  1. Once completely combined add your cream and whisk till smooth.

Store in the fridge if not using immediately.
