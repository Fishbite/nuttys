Sweet and sour pork

Sweet and sour pork, whats not to love? Take away versions are great, but, home made will wipe the floor if you box clever. Cut your meat into bite-size cubes and marinade in the five spice powder overnight if possible, it will transform your sweet and sour pork amazingly!

Now this is bit like cooking Indian food; preparation is every thing! So get all your ingriedients out, grab some little pots and some storage containers. It'll be fine trust me ;-) I've broken this down into stages. You could also if you prefer blend the sauce down once you've cooked your vegetables. Set it a blender 'till it's just sauce and have it like the little pots that come from the chinese take away. I occassionally do this especially if doing with say Chicken legs.

Meat

  • 375g diced pork shoulder*
  • 4 tsp five spice powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper

*Marinate over night, when ready to cook add the ingriedients below to your marinate pot and shake.

  • Juice of one lemon & zest
  • 5cm piece of fresh ginger diced
  • 2 cloves garlic finely diced
  • 1 Tbls oil

for the Sauce

  • 1 Tbls butter
  • 1/2 white onion very finely diced
  • 5cm ginger finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic finely diced
  • 2 Tbls brown sugar
  • Juice from pineapple tin
  • 1 Tbls cornflour
  • Water
  • 1 Tbls Sriracha hot chilli sauce ( Optional )
  • 4 Tbls Shaohsing rice wine vinegar*
  • 1 OXO™ vegetable stock cube crumbled

*Substitue Shaohsing rice wine vinegar with dry white sherry or a white wine vinegar if you need to.

Vegetables

  • 4 small tomatoes quatered
  • 8 thick slices of cucumber
  • 425g tin pineapples quartered
  • 1/2 Bunch spring onions
  • 1 green chilli sliced
  • Pinch of salt and pepper

method

Preparation is everything in making this an easy dish to cook. Once everything is ready, that's the bulk of the work done and once you start cooking, the dish comes together very quickly. I have three sets of preparations for the pork: the dry marinade, the sauce ingriedients and then the fruit & veg ingredients. Here's what my kitchen looks like when I'm ready to roll!
