Chapti Flat Bread Recipe

Every curry benefits from a flat bread of one kind or another, but chaptis are quick and easy to make. If I call at my friends just before meal time, I'll often find the kids having fun mixing the chapati dough in a great big mixing bowl. Here I give you a mini recipe, just enough for two! They're great with curries that have loads of gravy and even better with dry dishes such as my lamb pea and mint curry check that out here: lamb peas & mint curry recipe

serves 2

  • 120g chapati flour
  • 80ml water
  • 5ml oil
  • Ghee or butter to grease

method

I'm using chapati flour for this recipe, but you could use a mixture of wholemeal and plain white flour if you can't get hold of it. Putting the mix together is no problem, but you do need to knead the dough for a while before you can shape it ready for cooking.

  1. Put your flour in a large mixing bowl
  1. Make a little well in the middle
  2. Add water a little at a time
  1. Bring the dough together with your fingers
  1. Add the oil
  2. Form a dough ball, which should be quite dry at this point
  1. Knead the dough on a clean surface or board
  2. The dough will become soft & elastic after about 7 mins

The dough should be soft and elastic but not sticky, dust with a little flour if necessary

  1. Roll into a sausage shape & cut into two
  1. Roll one of the balls out until as thin as a tortilla
  1. Lightly grease a large flat pan
  2. Put it onto a med-high heat
  3. When it starts to smoke, add a chapati
  4. Coat the other side with ghee or butter
  5. Flip the chapati over when it starts to swell
  1. You should have black spots on the cooked side
  1. Coat the other side with ghee or butter
