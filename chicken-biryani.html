chicken biryani

oven baked

Chicken Biryani is an easy and simple biryani to make but oh my word the flavours and exture are incredible. It features tasty flavorful rice with perfectly cooked chicken drumsticks. The biryani masala used in this recipe is the Chettinad Masala from the Chettinad Cuisine which adds an unique flavor to the biryani. This recipe is very forgiving and the taste and flavor of the biriyani can be adjusted by using less fresh chilli and chilli powder ( But you know I like stuff HOT ). Since the chicken pieces and rice are going to cooked in the same vessel this is a typical One-pot-Biryani recipe. This easy and simple biryani is made by first grinding the Chettinad Masala and sauteing the onions and tomatoes with this freshly ground masala and then chicken pieces are cooked until they are halfway done and then pre-soaked rice is added and cooked in the oven for 45 minutes @ 180c.

Now as always the toughest job here is always going to be those browned onions but they are required for Chicken Biryani Oven Baked. Another task that's not simple is removing the skin from drumsticks. But again this is essential, do not worry about the bone end as this has no meat on it. tab below to see a step-by-step guide on how to make Chicken Biryani Oven Baked along with some pictures as a guide.

serves 4 to 6

For the Chicken Biryani Masala powder

  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 2 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 3 Green cardamom
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 tsp whole cumin seeds

for making the Chicken Biryani

  • 2 Tbsp Oil
  • 2 Tbsp Ghee/ Clarified Butter
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 3 Cloves
  • 3 Cardamom
  • 3 small white onion(250g) finely sliced
  • 1 tsp salt into the onions
  • 1 Tbsp Ginger paste
  • 1 Tbsp Garlic paste
  • 3 green chilli's split
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 Tbsp Extra hot Chilli powder
  • 1 tomato sliced(150g)
  • 2 Tbls Mint leaves chopped
  • 3 Tbls Coriander leaves chopped
  • 1/2 cup Yogurt
  • 1kg Chicken drumsticks
  • 1 cup Basmati Rice soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1.5 cups of water
  • 1/2 tsp Salt into the water

method

First lets get our masala ready. In a frying pan dry roast the spices for the masala powder until they are fragrant. Do not burn or they will be bitter. Once they are cool grind them to a fine powder and set aside. Next organise your ingredients and get your oil & ghee onto heat. Place the rice into cold water and allow to soak for 30 minutes. Preheat your oven when adding your chicken drumsticks to the pan.

  1. To make biryani masala - dry roast bay leaf, fennel seeds, coriander seeds, mace, star anise, cinnamon stick, pepper, Green cardamom, cloves & cumin seeds for 2 minutes on a medium heat cool completely and grind it to a fine powder
  1. Get your rice onto soak as it takes 30 minutes
  2. Heat a heavy based pan (on this occassion I am using my wok then transfering to my dutch oven to bake the dish)
  3. To begin the biryani - heat 2 Tbsp of oil and 2 Tbsp ghee / clarified butter (ghee adds extra aroma and richness to the biryani)
  4. then add the whole spices - bay leaves, cardamom and cloves.
  1. Add thinly sliced onion (its important to slice them thinly as you can), then add 1 tsp salt and saute until the onions are golden brown.
  1. Once the onions are nice and brown add 3 slit green chilies, 2 Tbsp of ginger garlic paste and cook until the raw smell is gone
  1. Now add the masalas - turmeric powder, freshly ground biryani masala (from step 1) nutmeg & chilli powder - this can be reduced to your spice preference. Give it a good stir fry (be careful do not burn the spice powders).
  1. Add sliced tomatoes and cook until the tomatoes soften down & begins to disappear (we don't want tomato chunks in our biryani) — mix well
  1. Now remove the skin from the chicken drumsticks if you have not already done so. Set your oven onto 180c to pre-heat
  2. Add the chicken drumsticks. Mix well, cover and cook until the chicken pieces are 50% done. About 5 minutes.
  1. Add some mint and coriander leaves, 1/2 cup of yogurt and mix everything well.
  1. Now add the cleaned, soaked & drained basmati rice on top of the chicken.
  1. Then add water (for 1 cup of Basmati rice add 1.5 cups of water). Add another 1/2 tsp salt. Cover & place in a pre-heated oven for 45 minutes.
  1. Uncover & check the rice is cooked — mix gently to enjoy tasty home-made Chettinad style chicken biryani.
