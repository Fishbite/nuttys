Indian Shepards pie

If you love traditional Shepards Pie and also have a passion for a little spice this dish will be right up your street. The addition of fresh chilli's and some store cupboard spices will transform you comfort food into an Indian dinner. This is so easy to make, it may well become a goto version of classic with an Indian twist. It is healthier than my Lamb Mash Pie as it is topped with mashed potatoe & butternut squash made with out butter or milk.

serves 4

For the Indian Shepards Pie mince

  • 2 Tbls oil
  • 1 green chilli slit
  • 1 red chilli slit
  • 1 Tbls ginger paste
  • 1 Tbls garlic paste
  • 250g onions finely chopped
  • 2 carrots peeled & grated
  • 2 celary sticks finely diced
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper powder
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp ground coriander powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin powder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 2 teaspoons tomato paste
  • 475 grams lamb mince
  • 2 Tbls yogurt, whisked
  • 1 Tbls chopped coriander
  • 100g frozen green peas

Optional Mushroom layer

  • 150g chestnut mushrooms
  • 2 teaspoons oil
  • 2 teaspoons butter
  • salt & pepper to taste

For the mash topping

  • 350g potatoes in skins
  • 450g Butternut squash peeled & diced
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 30g grated parmesan or mozzarella cheese
  • salt & pepper to taste

method

This recipe is a four stage process cook your mince, cook your optional layer of mushrooms, cook your potatoes & butternut squash in the microwave, mash both & assemble your pie. So it is important to be organised with your ingriedients prepare well & you are bound to succeed. ;-)I served this with a simple mint raita

Mince method

  1. Heat a heavy based pan add the oil once hot add the green & red chillies on a low heat till they look fried.
  2. Then add the ginger and garlic paste and saute for a few seconds.
  3. Add the chopped onions and fry till they turn translucent.
  4. Add your carrots & celary fry till they have softened & reduced in volume
  5. Then add the tomato paste and fry for a few seconds.
  6. Reduce the heat completely and add the spice powders. Fry for a few seconds before adding the minced meat.
  7. Fry the meat on a medium high heat till the colour deepens, around 3-4 minutes.
  8. Then add the whisked yogurt, salt to taste and chopped coriander leaves and continue to cook on medium heat till the meat is well browned.
  9. Add the frozen peas and continue to cook for another minute. Remove from heat and keep aside

mushroom layer method

  1. To make the optional mushroom layer, wash and cut the mushrooms into thin slices.
  2. Heat a saucepan and add the butter & oil in it. Add the sliced mushrooms and fry them on a high heat till they soften and shrink in size.
  3. Season with salt & pepper. Remove from heat & keep aside.

Mash method

We are basically going to bake our potatoes in there skins then remove the skins & discard. I vaccume packed my squash pricked some holes in the bag & microwaved. You can simply cover with cling film if you do not have a vaccume pack machine and prick some holes in the film.

  1. Cook both your potatoes & squash separately in the microwave
  2. 5 minutes per potatoe as a rough guide
  3. 5 minutes for the diced butternut squash again this is a rough guide
  4. Once tender drain any liquid & mash them into a pan on a low heat.
  5. Add the egg yolk, salt, white pepper & grated parmesan cheese and mash everything together again.

Assemble the pie

  1. To assemble, grease a 9X9 inch square or 9 inch round ceramic or glass, oven safe dish with butter.
  2. Add the cooked mince mixture and level off the surface.
  3. Top it up with the optional mushroom layer or directly add the mashed potato & butternut squash mixture
  4. Level off the surface of the mashed potatoes & butternut squash mixture,then run a fork through the surface to form a pretty design.
  5. place the dish into the air fryer @ 180c for 15 to 20 minutes if using a conventional oven see method below
  6. Preheat an oven at 180 degrees C and bake for 12 minutes.
  7. Then change the oven setting to 'grill' mode and grill for 3-4 minutes or till the pie gets a beautiful golden crust.
  8. Remove, cool slightly and then gently run a knife through to create 4 equal portions.
  9. Serve hot with a cooling mint raita
