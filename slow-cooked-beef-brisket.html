ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

serves 4

  • 2 x sprays oil
  • 1 kg Beef Brisket Joint
  • 4 medium (300g) carrots cut into chucks
  • 1 large (150g) parsnip cut into chucks
  • 3 sticks (100) celary cut into chucks
  • 1 large (200g) white onion cut into chucks
  • 1 1/2 tsp Sea salt roughly ground
  • 2 tsp Black peppercorn roughly ground
  • 2 beef OXO cubes crumbled
  • 1 veg OXO cube crumbled
  • 2 tsp English mustard powder
  • 3 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp Worschester sauce or Hendersons relish
  • 750 ml Boiling water
  • 1/2 Tbls oil or butter
  • 1/2 Tbls plain flour
  • 1 Tbls bisto powder*

*enough cold water to form a slurry

method

I am using an electric slow cooker for my Slow Cooked Beef Brisket but if you do not have one of those this recipe is probably not for you :-) Sorry just kidding you can achieve the dish with a pressure cooker far quicker but for timing that be quided by your brand. Now this is really fast to put together & really, really, no really slow to cook 10 to 12 hours and an hour resting required for the meat. I am not even going to peel the carrots something rarely stated in Nuttys kitchen! No fine dicing or exact sizes just large dice for all the vegetables so it is gonna be a breeze.

The most important task is sealing & browning our meat in a really hot pan I have my stir-fry ring on max to achieve that quickly. So lets get all our vegetables prepped & our seasoning out, weighed, ground and do this ting.

So excited about tomorrows dinner mashed potatoes & sweetheart cabbage all prepared.....12 ish hrs of smelling that pot is either going to have me salavating or drive me completely Nuts:-(

(Edit from IT "To late Nutty, that days long past!")

  1. So fistly place your carrot chucks into the bottom of your slow cooker.
    • 4 medium (300g) carrots cut into chucks
    • 1 large (150g) parsnip cut into chucks
    • 3 sticks (100) celary cut into chucks
    • 1 large (200g) white onion cut into chucks
  2. Add in your parsnips chunks on top & then your celary chunks lastly add onions
  3. Place 750 ml of water into the kettle ready to boil. Check your seasoning are all ready to go.
  4. Now remove packaging from the Beef Brisket pat the joint dry with kitchen paper.
  5. Place a heavy based frying pan on your fastest ring set to max and once smoking spray twice with spray oil.
  6. Find the place on the joint with the most fat and set in down into the pan try not to move it till you can hear it browned.
  7. Then turn and continue section by section untill you have achieved a deap crisp brown all arround the Joint then brown both flat ends.
  8. Turn the ring off and place the Joint on top of all your vegetables.
  9. Top the joint and vegetables with Salt & papper we prepared earlier
    • 1 1/2 tsp Sea salt roughly ground
    • 2 tsp Black peppercorn roughly ground
  10. Boil the kettel add 750 ml of the boiling water into your meat frying pan and add your all your seasonings.
    • 2 beef OXO cubes crumbled
    • 1 veg OXO cube crumbled
    • 2 tsp English mustard powder
    • 3 tsp smoked paprika
    • 1 tsp Worschester sauce or Hendersons relish
  11. Stir untill the stock has disolved then carefully pour the liquid arround the side of the meat leaving the meat untouched.
  12. Set on high for 1 hour then reduce to low for 10hrs*.

*Please do not be tempted to 'Peek' everytime you open the lid your adding 30 minutes to the cooking time, as you have released the heat.

  1. 10hrs later.....So our beef should be melt in the mouth done.
  2. Carefully remove the joint to a plate to rest for 1 hour.
  3. Now we need to thicken the gravy so get your remaining ingriedients.
    • 1/2 Tbls oil or butter
    • 1/2 Tbls plain flour
    • 1 Tbls bisto powder*
  4. Get a small pan and melt the oil or butter add in the flour and stir to cook out the flour.
  5. Get a small pot of water and add the bisto powder stir untill smooth & lump free.
  6. Now combine both and add to the slow cooker stir through and turn the slow cooker to high to thicken up as the meat continues to rest. I now start boiling my potatoes for my mash. Praying that it was all worth the wait??? = The answer is it was Nutty to ADD REASON

Serving suggestion.

Has to be creamy mashed potatoes and sweet heart cabbage to accompany my Slow Cooked Beef Brisket but with all this time to concider it might become horseraddish mash!
