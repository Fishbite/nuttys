Mum's Fish Pie

Okay! So fish is way more expensive to the point of unaffordable these days. Tiny little bits from a supermarket are not what my mum used. But I am going to recreate the classic she served in her pub. Using a few tricks to bulk the fish out. Butter, veg and mashed potatoes. She literally used softened onions, white sauce, lots of cod — huge chunks of fish, a basic white sauce and a few peas and parsley. So how do we do that nowadays without buying a ready meal, which admittedly are cheaper? But, contain no real fish!! Difficult, Yup ya have no idea! But this is my answer to create Mum's Fish Pie on a budget. I managed to get 4 pieces of frozen Atlantic wild cod for less than four quid (£4) so that makes it reasonable if I can make it serve four.

method

All the veg' needs to be cut to a very fine dice so that you never really know they're there in the final dish. Now, all we are after is a rich base sauce and if we make that on the stove top in a pan, it's going to take ages and ages :-( but, I want this to be something that you can put together a little more quickly. So, with that thought, I have my trusty microwave to hand! I'll soften the onions celery and leeks with a knob of butter in the oven-proof dish that I'm going to bake the Fish Pie in. I could just make a parsley sauce, but I find the dish really benefits from a vegetable base to elevate to another level completely. So, start with your veg' base and then create the parsley sauce in the same dish, then and only then, put your potatoes on to boil for 20 minutes. After that, turn to your fish. The packet says 'cook from frozen' so, I lay the fish on baking paper on top of tin foil, wrap it up to keep the moisture in and then pop it in the oven until its thawed, but not quite cooked. How long for? 20 to 25 minutes @180c as a guide, but that really depends on the thickness of the fish, I checked mine after 20mins and it was perfect, completely thawed, but not quite cooked, which is exactly how you want it because we're going to fully cook it in the pie.

So, this is the idea, we have hot mash ready, hot sauce ready and fish ready to cook. With everything hot, it's really not going to take long to finish it off in the oven, So, break the fish up into chunks on top of the sauce and then give it a good mix before topping it off with the creamy mashed potatoes. Pop it in the oven for a while to brown the mash and cook the fish fully, and there you have it! My version of my mum's Fish Pie albeit, with not quite as much fish as she would have used but, with all the flavour that made this a family favourite and a smash hit with her customers.

One important note here, seasoning is key, so taste everything as you go. And don't forget to season your fish with a good pinch of salt and pepper, it's really gonna need it to achieve that classic taste.
