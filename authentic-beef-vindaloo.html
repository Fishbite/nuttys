Authentic Beef Vindaloo

Well this is definately an Authentic recipe Nutty did not invent this, I've never had it in this country and in India you do not see Beef on the menu traditionally it would be made with Pork. Until James Martin's guest made it I was so excited my mind raced to India and my taste buds screamed you must make it now! So my next shopping trip I brought braising steak. Do not be daunted it is a chefs recipe but I have tried to break it down use accesssable ingriedients and indeed methods. The only change I have listed as optional is we found it a bit too on the mild side so added 2 tsp of extra hot chilli powder then we found it perfects ;-). Break it down into 3 sets of tasks make the paste day before make the vindaloo masala at the same time then you will have two little pots of majic all ready. You will need to dice your beef and marinade for 24 hrs in the vindaloo paste ready to cook the next day. Curtercy of Aktar Islam - Thank you Aktar for sharing this with us, Spiced Braised beef in vindaloo paste with coconut, tamarind and curry leaves. It is a myth that vindaloo contains potatoes! Vinegar and garlic should always be the base.

ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

serves 4

*Vindaloo paste x 2

*This paste is enough for 1kg of meat so we only need half of it!

  • 18g Kashmiri chilli
  • 100g Garlic
  • 60g Ginger
  • 150ml white wine vinegar
  • 10g Black pepper
  • 14 cloves
  • 5g cinnamon stick
  • 8 Cardamom pods
  • 6g mustard seeds
  • 7g cumin seeds
  • 13g coriander seeds
  • 2g Turmeric
  • *2 tsp extra hot chilli powder

* optional

Vindaloo Garam Masala (powdered)

*This powder is enough for multipy dishes so we only need a tsp of it!

  • 15g Cardamom
  • 5g Cloves
  • 7g cinnamon

Beef vindaloo

  • 500g Beef braising steak cut in to 3” pieces
  • 100ml coconut oil
  • 300g = aproximately 2 diced brown onions
  • 2 ripe tomatoes chopped
  • 1/4 Tbsp tamarind
  • 5g Jaggery or 1 tsp sugar to taste
  • Pack of curry fresh leaves
  • 1 OXO beef stock cube
  • 1/4 ltr water
  • 37.5ml thick coconut milk

Method brief guide to the three stages ;-)

Vindaloo paste

First lets get the paste ready you will need a small frying pan, pestle & mortar or spice grinder and a small blender. Find a sealed pot large enough for all your meat and half your paste as this is enough for two curries. Gather all your ingriedients measure them and keep in order

Vindaloo masala

The same frying pan pestle and mortar or spice grinder. A sealed pot to keep the masala in as this is enough for 4 to 5 recipes

Beef Vindaloo

Chose a non stick pan suitable to brown everything I then switch to an oven proff dish when ready to slow cook. The onions are going to take a while as we do need them well browned. Okay lets do this ting ;-)

Vindaloo paste

  1. So plastic or glass container to marinade in add 150ml of white wine vinegar to it and 18g chilli's shut with a lid as you peel your garlic and ginger once done add them into the pot.
  2. Now your spices pre-heat an empty frying pan then add 10g Black pepper, 14 cloves, 5g cinnamon stick, 8 Cardamom pods, 6g mustard seeds, 7g cumin seeds and 13g coriander seeds gently warm through untill it smells fragrant.
  3. Tip them into a pestle and mortar to cool
  4. Add 2g turmeric and one or two tsp extra hot chilli powder if using to your marinade pot.
  5. Once your whole spices have cooled give them a bash and set into your spice grinder. Grind to a powder then transfere to a mini blender.
  6. Add your garlic and ginger soaked to the blender.
  7. Blitz well till smooth set half the paste into a sealed jar and the other half into your marinade pot.
  8. Add 1 kg of beef to your spice paste mixture stir well & shake then set into the fridge for 24hrs.

Vindaloo masala

  1. Now your spices pre-heat an empty frying pan then add 15g Cardamom, 5g Cloves and 7g cinnamon
  2. Once they small fragrant set them into a pestle and mortar to cool
  3. Once cooled give them a bash and set into your spice grinder. Once completely powder set into a jar and label.

Beef Vindaloo

  1. So 24 hours have passed we have some more to prepare but first lets get our meat out the fridge to warm and do our onions
  2. Heat oil in a heavy based pot, add onions and curry leaves and caramelise till golden. about 20 minutes
  3. Now turn the pan to highest heat add the marinated beef along with all the marinade in the bowl, wash the bowl down with a little water and add this to the pot along with the tomatoes.
  4. Stir fry the beef untill oil separates
  5. Add the jaggery or sugar, another handful of curry leaves, coconut milk and cook out again till oil separates.
  6. Now pour in beef stock cube, cover and simmer on low heat for 2 hours, keep stirring occasionally so it doesn't catch.
  7. Once 2 hrs are up and the beef is tender, uncover turn the heat to medium and reduce the sauce until its thickened to your liking and the oil separates.
  8. Top 1 tsp of the vindaloo masala, check seasoning and adjust as necessary and serve with any indian bread or rice.
