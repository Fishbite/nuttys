Smoked Haddock and Leek Quiche

Who does not love a Pie! But Quiche is another level basically a lidless Pie. Not a stone cold solid thick pastry base with a tasteless filling like you get in the supermarkets. A freshly baked Quiche warm still from the oven. A delicate thin pastry base which is loaded with leeks, onion, double cream, eggs and seasoned to perfection! This is like the best cheese sauce in a PIE loaded with leeks & Smoked Haddock! Years ago I made this filling with just Smoked Haddock a little onion and cheese here I have tried to make it more affordable with the addition of leeks. Those were the days when a dish did not cost an arm and a leg... I hope you give it a go and enjoy the results.

serves 8

Pastry Base

  • 320g Shop brought butter pastry
  • 800g rice or flour to blind bake
  • 1 beaten egg to glaze the base

Filling

  • 3 whole eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 250ml double cream
  • 1 Tbls Vegetable oil
  • 50g salted butter
  • 1 tsp brown mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp nigella seeds (onion seeds)
  • 200g finely diced onion
  • 300g washed & sliced leeks
  • 1 tsp black pepper corns crushed
  • 1 tsp sea salt crushed
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp english mustrad powder
  • 1 tsp Dyjon mustard
  • 160g Strong Cheddar cheese grated
  • 330g Frozen or fresh Smoked Haddock
  • 4 halved cherry or small vine tomatoes to garnish

method

Our first job is too get our pastry base blind baked pre-heat the oven to 180c and greese your flan dish with a little butter. Then we can line our dish with the pastry tucking it in gently to all the corners and prick all over with a fork. Leave any over-hang this prevents your pastry shrinking below the rim of the dish. Next take a round of greese proof larger than the flan dish cover the flan dish and fill with rice or flour. I use rice store it away till the next PIE day arrives. Whelst it pre bakes you will have time too slice and wash your leeks. Finely dice your onions and prepare the seasonings. Lastly we will crack our eggs do our yolks and whisk then add cream stir it through. Be sure to keep a small amount of pastry aside just incase you have any holes during baking you can then plug the hole with a little fresh pastry!

  1. Bake the base @ 180c for 10 minutes then remove the rice and greese proff paper and return to the oven for another 10 minutes.
  2. Egg wash the entire base and sides.
  3. Heat a heavy based pan with 1 Tbls Oil and 50g butter.
  4. Add in 1 tsp brown mustard seeds 1 tsp Nigella seeds allow to sizzle.
  5. Add in 200g finely diced onions and 300g finely diced leeks. Allow to soften with out colour. Do not use a lid as we need them both to dry out as much as possibe.
  6. Add in all your seasoning ingriedients 1 tsp of each pepper, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, english mustard and Dyjon mustard and stir through
  7. Pour the mixture into your flan dish spread evenly across the base.
  8. *I use 330g frozen fish which meant I had to poach it gently for 6 minutes in a little milk. Then remove the skin and top your leek mixture with the fish.

*If using fresh fish simply flake dirrectly into the flan dish raw.

  1. Top the fish with 160g strong cheddar cheese.
  2. Decorate with halved cherry tomatoes 1/2 per slice is a good rule of thumb ;-)
  3. Place the flan dish onto the oven rack and use a jug to pour your cream and egg mixture into the flan dish. Do this slowly as if it runs over you are going to have a big mess!
  4. Bake @ 180c for 30 to 35 minutes depending on the flan dish mines old fashioned heavy thick earthen ware so 30 minutes will be enough as it will continue to bake in the dish. If using a thin tin dish 35 minutes will porbably be required.
  5. Carefully trim the excess pastry with a sharp knife
  6. Allow to cool for 30 minutes to nearly room temperature to enable slicing & serve.

Serving suggestion

Smoked Haddock and Leek Quiche

A simple dressed salad and a few chunky chips or a thick slice simply served with hot Baked Beans. ;-)

Nutty Tip - If you have any left overs, chill in the fridge and give a quick blast in the microwave to warm or remove from the fridge an hour before you wish to serve as fridge cold Quiche tastes of nothing a bit like those ones we discussed earlier.
