Bengali Chicken Kosha

To say this is a curry does not do this dish justice. In fact this is an aromatic blend of sweet, sour and spicy — and it is so indulgent.

Firstly you are going to need Mustard oil! No exceptions no substitutes! Vegetable oil is simply not going to cut it — as soon as the Mustard Oil hits the hot pan, that completely amazing smell that fills the room will convince you — There is no substitute!

Please be wary of what you buy as most Mustard Oil in the UK is not for cooking with.

It is for external use only and should not be eaten, so read the lable carefully before buying.

I used a SaraLiving™ Cold pressed Mustard Oil from that very well known internet delivery company. Not cheap @£13.99 for 500ml but once you have tried it you will never complain about the cost.

Now I have specifically used Chicken on the bone for this recipe, but you can use any meat or indeed fish — just adjust your cooking times accordingly.

Another slight warning — as the mustard oil heats - try to step away from the pan as it does catch the back of your throat and makes your eyes smart a litte. But it is delicious trust me!

Click the Method tab below to see a step-by-step guide on how to make Bengali Chicken Kosha along with some pictures as a guide.

ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

serves 4

ingredients marinade

  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp extra hot chilli powder
  • 1 Tbsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp sea salt - crushed
  • 5 cloves garlic - roughly chopped
  • 2 inch ginger - roughly chopped
  • 2 green chilli - roughly chopped
  • 1 Tbsp plain yogurt
  • 2 Tbsp mustard oil
  • 1 kg skinless bone in chicken

ingredients curry base

  • 63 ml mustard oil
  • 500g sliced red onions
  • ½ tsp jaggery or soft brown sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 3 Tbsp yogurt
  • ½ tsp kashmiri chilli powder
  • ½ tsp gram flour
  • 1 tsp ghee
  • 250ml water

ingredients Garam masala

  • 5 cloves
  • 3 green cardamon
  • 1 black cardamon
  • ½ inch cinnamon
  • 10 black peppercorns
  • 1 piece Mace
  • ½ tsp coriander
  • 2 dried red chillies
  • ¼ tsp cumin
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg

method

As I am sure you noticed by the ingredients lists, I have broken this down into 3 stages:

  • Marinade
  • Curry paste
  • & finishing Garam masala

I make them all the night before so the next day all I have to do is assemble & cook the dish!

For the marinade I simple place everything into a mini blender, blitz, done, then just add chicken!

For the garam masala — heat a pan, warm all the whole spices till fragrant, cool, grind and add the nutmeg powder — done!

Now all we really have to do is our curry, which has well browned onions — 20 minutes of life we shall never gets back! I know but it is worth it! Tis okay calms.... We are calms ;-). Big are on, lets do this ting!

Spice mariande

Our first task is to get the spice marinade made so get a container large enough for your meat & marinade — Plastic or glass

Do not use a metal container!

  1. To keep yourself sane, get the ingredients ready!
    • ½ tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp extra hot chilli powder
    • 1 Tbsp coriander powder
    • 1 tsp sea salt crushed
    • 5 cloves garlic roughly chopped
    • 2 inch ginger roughly chopped
    • 2 green chillies roughly chopped
    • 1 Tbsp plain yogurt
    • 2 Tbsp mustard oil
  1. I use a mini blender to chop everything to a paste as it takes seconds, but this can be done in a pestle and mortar
  2. Now slash into the meat of your chicken twice & add into the spice paste container
    • 1 kg skinless bone in chicken

Finishing garam masala

  1. Heat a small dry frying pan and place all your whole spices listed below & toast gently until they smell fragrant.
    • 5 cloves
    • 3 green cardamon
    • 1 black cardamon
    • ½ inch cinnamon
    • 10 black peppercorns
    • 1 piece Mace
    • ½ tsp coriander
    • 2 dried red chillies
    • ¼ tsp cumin
  1. Now set them aside to cool. Once totally cooled grind to a fine powder and add the pre-ground spice below.
    • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg

making the curry

  1. Organise the ingredients for the curry:
    • 63 ml mustard oil
    • 500g sliced red onions
    • ½ tsp jaggery or soft brown sugar
    • ½ tsp salt
    • 3 Tbsp yogurt
    • ½ tsp kashmiri chilli powder
    • ½ tsp gram flour
    • 1 tsp ghee
    • 250ml water
  1. Get a heavy based pan, I use my wok & heat the oil till smoking hot, lower the heat to medium, add your onions, salt & sugar stir / turn occassionally for about 20 minutes.
  1. Add the following to the yogurt & set aside.
    • 3 Tbsp yogurt
    • ½ tsp kashmiri chilli powder
    • ¾ tsp garam masala
    • ½ tsp gram flour
    • 1 tsp ghee
  1. Once your onions are completely browned add your chicken & all the marinade & stir fry for 5 minutes on high heat.
  1. Add your yogurt spice blend & 250ml water, stir to combine, bring to hot, cover & lower to a gentle simmer — cook for 20 minutes.
  2. Remove the lid & continue to cook for another 10 minutes till liquid has reduced.
  3. Check the seasoning & add in the last ¼ of the garam masala mix.

Serve immediately!

If you re-heat this in a microwave later all the oils will release from the sauce, so it is at it's best when just cooked or re-heated with care and a little caution slowly in a pan.

Serving Suggestion A simple red onion rice will do quite nice :-)
