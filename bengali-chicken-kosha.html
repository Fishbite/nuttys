Bengali Chicken Kosha

Too say this is a curry does not do this dish justice. In fact this is a blend of sweet sour spicy fragrant and so indulgent. Firstly you are going to need Mustard oil! No exceptions no substitutes! Vegetable oil is simply not going to cut it the majority of the flavour for this dish comes from the Mustard Oil and please be wary of what you buy as most in the UK is not Mustard Oil for cooking with it is for skin and hair and should not be eaten. I used a SaraLiving Cold pressed Mustard Oil from that very well known internet delivery shop. Not cheap @£13.99 for 500ml but once you have tried it you will never complain about the cost. Now I have specifically used Chicken on the bone but you can use any meat or indeed fish just adjust your cooking times accordingly.

Another slight warning as the mustard oil heats - try to step away from the pan as it does catch the back of your throat and makes your eye smart a litte. But it is delicious trust me.

Click the Method tab below to see a step-by-step guide on how to make Bengali Chicken Kosha along with some pictures as a guide.

ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

serves 4

ingredients marinade

  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp extra hot chilli powder
  • 1 Tbls corriander powder
  • 1 tsp sea salt crushed
  • 5 cloves garlic roughly chopped
  • 2 inch ginger roughly chopped
  • 2 green chilli roughly chopped
  • 1 Tbls plain yougert
  • 2 Tbls mustard oil
  • 1 kg skinless bone in chicken

ingredients curry base

  • 62.5 ml mustard oil
  • 500g sliced red onions
  • 1/2 tsp jaggery or soft brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3 Tbls yougert
  • 1/2 tsp kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp gram flour
  • 1 tsp ghee
  • 250ml water

ingredients Garam masala

  • 5 cloves
  • 3 green cardamon
  • 1 black cardamon
  • 1/2 inch cinnamon
  • 10 black peppercorns
  • 1 piece Mace
  • 1/2 tsp corriander
  • 2 dried red chilli's
  • 1/4 tsp cumin
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg

method

As I am sure you noticed by the ingredients lists, I have broken this down into 3 stages. Marinade Curry paste & finishing Garam masala I do all the night before then the next day all I have to do is assemble & cook the dish. So the marinade simple I place everything into a mini blender blitz done add chicken! The Garam masala heat pan warm all the whole spices till fragrant cool grind and add the nutmeg powder done! Now all we really have to do is our curry which has well browned onions 20 minutes of life we shall never gets back I know but it is worth it's tis okay calms.... we are calms ;-)Big Pants are on lets do dis ting!

Spice mariande

  1. Our first task is to get the spice marinade made so get a container large enough for your meat & marinade. Plastic or glass do not use metal.
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp extra hot chilli powder
  • 1 Tbls corriander powder
  • 1 tsp sea salt crushed
  • 5 cloves garlic roughly chopped
  • 2 inch ginger roughly chopped
  • 2 green chilli roughly chopped
  • 1 Tbls plain yougert
  • 2 Tbls mustard oil
  1. I use a mini blender to chop every thing to a paste as it takes seconds but this can be done in a pestle and mortar
  2. Now slice into the meat of your chicken slightly & add into the spice paste container
  • 1 kg skinless bone in chicken

Finishing garam masala

  1. Heat a small dry frying pan and place all your whole spices listed below toast untill they smell fragrant
  • 5 cloves
  • 3 green cardamon
  • 1 black cardamon
  • 1/2 inch cinnamon
  • 10 black peppercorns
  • 1 piece Mace
  • 1/2 tsp corriander
  • 2 dried red chilli's
  • 1/4 tsp cumin
    Now set them aside to cool once totally cooled grind to a fine powder and add the preground spice below.
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg

Method for the curry base

  1. Get a heavy based pan I use my wok heat 225ml till smoking hot lower the heat to medium add you onions 1/2 tsp salt & 1/4 tsp sugar stir / turn occassionally for about 20 minutes.
  2. Into your 3 Tbls of yougert add the 1/2 tsp gram flour 1/2 tsp kashmiri chilli powder 3/4 of your garam masala & 1 tsp ghee set aside.
  3. Once your onions are completely browned add your chicken & all the marinade stir fry for 5 minutes on high heat.
  4. Add your yougert spice blend & 250ml water stir to combine bring to hot cover lower to a gentle simmer and cook for 20 minutes.
  5. Remove the lid and continue to cook for another 10 minutes till liquid has reduced.
  6. Check the seasoning and add in the last 1/4 of the garam masala mix.
  7. Serve immediately if you re-heat this in a microwave later all the oils will release from the dish. So it is at it's best when just cooked or reheated with care and a little caution slowly in a pan.
