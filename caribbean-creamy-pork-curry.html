Caribbean Creamy Pork Curry

Caribbean Creamy Pork Curry Now this is completely different to most of my Caribbean dishes, not a fresh chilli in sight for starters. Greek yogurt & coconut cream create a delicate sauce. I always have a Caribbean spice mix in my cupboard it's so easy to make a spice paste with & the perfect blend of spices for this dish. I know you'll Love it! I served this with baked potatoes & salad but rice would be a perfect partner too. I love the way the spices work with the yogurt & coconut cream to create a creamy sauce that is full of flavour. The pork is tender and juicy, and the peppers add a nice crunch to the dish. It's perfect for a quick weeknight dinner. Give it a try and let me know what you think! Enjoy! :¬)

ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

Serves 4

Ingredients to marinade

  • 450g Pork leg steaks diced
  • 1 lime - juiced
  • 5 garlic cloves - crushed
  • 3 cm fresh ginger - crushed
  • 3 Tbsp Dunns River Hot Caribbean curry powder
  • 1 tsp extra hot chilli powder (Optional)
  • 3 tablespoons greek yogurt
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil

Ingredients curry

  • 3 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 4 med white onions - halved & sliced
  • 1 tsp sea salt ground
  • 1 tsp black pepper corns crushed (optional)
  • 1 x 450g tin of plum tomatoes crushed
  • 450g greek yogurt
  • 65g creamed coconut cream
  • 1 red bell pepper sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper sliced

method

So our first task is to get the paste done once your limes juiced garlic & ginger are crushed you can set all 7 ingredients into a plastic container. Add your diced pork & set into the fridge preferably over night. Now this is an easy dish to cook once you have got your paste ready and meat marinaded. Just slice your onions while the pan heats & slice your peppers while the dish simmers. I cooked this in my dutch oven on the hob but any heavy based pan will be fine

Method for the marinade

  1. juice your lime into a pestle & mortar
  2. add your garlic cloves & ginger, bash to a paste
  3. add your curry powder & chilli powder if using
  4. add your vegetable oil & combine
  5. get a plastic pot with a lid & add your spice paste
  6. add yogurt & your diced pork
  7. give it all a very good mix
  8. seal the container & set into a fridge overnight

Method for the curry

  1. Heat a heavy based pan & add oil
  2. once hot add your onions to soften & brown just a little
  3. now add your marinated meat - have a lid to hand as it could spit at first
  4. remove the lid after it settles add salt & pepper brown your meat pieces & spices
  5. once browned add the tomatoes, your yogurt & creamed coconut
  6. lower the heat cover with a tight fitting lid & cook for 30 minutes
  7. check if the meat is tender, if not continue to cook a for longer
  8. once tender add in your peppers & cook until peppers are just softening about 10 minutes uncovered
  9. you do want to retain some crunch to the peppers adding texture to the dish
