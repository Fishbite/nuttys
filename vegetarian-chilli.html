Vegetarian Chilli

I have a vegetarian guest here for a while causing me to have to think a little outside of my comfort zone. I saw a butternut squash in the shops and thought hmmm!? Vegetarian Chilli Con Carni! Of course that will work so, add in some courgettes ect... I have especially bought some Chipotle chillies as I some times use the paste & really want to know what they add to a dish. They smell a bit like a bonfire night does that work? Well not willing to test them out on expensive meat but still wanting an exciting dinner Vegetarian Chilli it is. This will involve a lot of chopping but once that is done the dish will easily fly together.

Click the Method tab below to see a step by step guide on how to make my Vegetarian Chilli along with a few pictures.

ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

serves about 10!

Just reduce the quantity of veg' and tinned stuff to make a smaller batch. The spice quantities can remain the same, with the exception of the extra hot chilli powder, maybe you might want to reduce that a touch or, maybe not! :¬P

Spice ingredients

  • 3 chipotle chilli's sliced
  • 1 tsp cumin seed
  • 1 & 1/2 tsp hot chilli powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp black pepper corns crushed
  • 1 tsp sea salt crushed*

*Add 1 tsp salt to begin the dish it may require more but taste near the end of cooking to ensure the dish is well seasoned!

Vegetable ingredients

  • 1 white onion sliced
  • 1 red onion sliced
  • 2 carrots grated
  • 3 sticks celery sliced finely
  • 4 cloves garlic crushed
  • 300g potatoes peeled washed & cubed
  • 1 Butternut squash pealed & cubed
  • 200g 1 or 2 Aubergines cubed
  • 225g 1 or 2 courgettes cubed
  • 125g sweetcorn
  • red bell pepper cubed
  • green bell pepper cubed
  • yellow bell pepper cubed
  • 1 large lemon wedged into 4

Store cupboard ingredients

  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 400g 2 tin plum tomatoes mashed
  • 400g 2 tin tomato soup
  • 390g tin of kidney beans
  • 400g tin of cannellini beans
  • 2 x vegetable stock cubes crumbled

method

First get all your ingriedients out & ready, chopping is laborious but an even dice is key for every thing to cook uniformly. It also effects the look of any dish so take your time. Keep all the vegetables separate as we will add in stages. Set the aubergine courgettes & butternut squash into cold water with a wedge of lemon as you go as it tends to discolour.

chopped veg'

I actually pop my diced potatoes, butternut squash, courgettes and aubergines in a hot oven for 45 minuted first, just to give a little roasted flavour to the final dish. So, if you want to do this, just lay them out in trays, drizzle all over with a little olive oil and then sprinkle with salt and pepper.

chopped veg' to roast

I get all my spices ready along with the tinned ingredients so that I can fly through putting the dish together. This dish is all about prep' as usual! There's a lotta choppin' but it'll all be over in the flash of a good knife!

spices & tinned ingredients
  1. Heat a large wide heavy based pan onto the hob and allow to heat
  2. Once hot add oil & wait 'till it shimmers
  3. Add your cumin seeds soon as they begin to sizzle add in your white onion
  4. Stir fry 'till the onions begin to soften then add your garlic & red onion
  5. Once softened add in your spice mixture, chilli's & stock cubes, stir fry to toast the spice mix
  6. Add your grated carrots & celery, stir fry to combine until the carrots reduce by two thirds
  7. Now create a well in the center, add in your potatoes & stir fry to combine
  8. add in your butternut & stir fry to combine
  9. add in your courgettes & stir fry to combine
  10. add in your aubergines & stir fry to combine
  11. add in your peppers & stir fry to combine
  12. add in your mushrooms & stir fry to combine
  13. Now we can add in all our tinned ingreidients
  14. Soon as they all heat up reduce the temperature & cover for 10 minutes
  15. Now it's over to you on your preference of how you like your peppers cooked
  16. Test the seasoning & check the cook on the vegetables
vegetarian chilli check the veg & seasoning
served with a warm buttered baggette it's a totally meat free treat

Not Suitable For Freezing ...as the veg' will turn to mush!
