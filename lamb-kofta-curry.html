Lamb Kofta Curry

The recipe for Lamb Kofta Curry is actually a Pakistani version and not Indian. Nowadays this is goto dish for me when I want to treat me, yup just me lol. As it is like a kebab in a delicate curry sauce, not a fried kofta or kebab though. I have had that version & just for starters you need a bottle of OIL just to fry your koftas! So on the hunt for another way a few years ago, I discovered another technique! Place your koftas (kebabs) as you would do meat into a curry sauce. I am not saying this dish is easy, but at least your home does not smell like a fish & chip shop! Less oil, still flavour full to the max, what is not to love. Prepare, prepare, double check, take a breath & then cook with a smile! I have laid it out in 3 stages.... Yes I said your going to need to focus, 30 mins prep & about 50 mins to cook. method

This dish really benefits from resting over night in the fridge. But I would advise you to portion it into individual portions as this can be difficult once chilled. If you have a family to feed, or guests, just reheat & serve.

Method Meatballs

  1. Set your chilli's garlic & ginger into your food processor process '-until finely chopped
  2. Add the onion coriander & mint in. Use the pulse function to chop so that the onions are finely chopped but not blended.

You don't want the onions to break down too much and release water.

  1. Add the rest of the ingredients listed under meatballs and process for 20-30 seconds or just until mixed.*
  2. I roll immediately into two sausage shaped tubes of cling film to the size I roughly want my kofta's to be. Set aside in the fridge for at least 3 to 12 hours wrapped in the cling film.
  3. Once you're ready & your sauce is on, using a bit of water to wet your hands so the meat does not stick to your hands, form into meatballs about 1 inch in diameter.
  4. It should make around 25 meatballs. Set aside back into the fridge if your not cooking immediately.

*The meat will change from red to a mid biege brown colour when done

Method Eggs

  1. This is easy! Boil them for 10 minutes & peel off the egg shells (ROFL)
  2. Once the shells are removed just pop them into your cooked curry to absorb the flavour.

method curry

  1. Heat oil in a large, heavy bottomed pan over medium-high heat. Add the cumin seeds and allow them to sizzle for a few seconds.
  2. Add the sliced onions & diced and sauté, stirring often for 10 minutes, or until softened & slightly browned
  3. Add the garlic and ginger, sauté for another 2 minutes until the onions have deepened a little more in colour.
  4. Add the tomatoes, spices and salt, stir fry until oil separates from the spices
  5. Add green chilli pepper followed by the yogurt
  6. Sauté for 4-5 minutes or until the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  7. Add 2 cups water and increase the heat to bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting.
  8. Once it has stopped boiling, arrange the koftas in a single layer.
  9. Increase the heat to medium, cover and allow it to cook for 10 minutes.
    10. "
  10. Uncover and gently stir the koftas. Reduce the heat to low-medium.
  11. Cover and allow it to simmer for another 35 minutes, stirring once in between until cooked through.
  12. Remove the lid after 20 minutes to evaporate water to make the kofta to desired consistency.
  13. Taste and add salt, if needed. Add in your garam masala coriander & whole boiled eggs while the sauce is still hot to infuse them in curry sauce
  14. Eat immediately or set into the fridge overnight for the flavours to infuse and you will have a perfect resturant style dinner tomorrow
