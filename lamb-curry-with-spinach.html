lamb curry with spinach

Lamb curry with spinach, or Saag Gosht as it is known is from the Punjab, it's delicate and delicious, not overly spiced or indeed complicated. If you can make a shepards pie you can make this — it's easier! Bell peppers add texture, colour and a lovely flavour. As always—I like to use more vegetables than meat if the dish will take it. The Spinach is added right at the end retaining it's vitamins, minerals, flavour and vibrant colour. Preparation is not so important here as you have 20 minutes while your onions brown and you can also make your spice paste. Then you have another 25 to 35 minutes while your meat is cooking and you can chop your vegetables then — Loads o' time! ;-)

ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

  • 3 Tbls vegetable oil
  • 1 white onion sliced
  • 1 red onion sliced
  • 300g diced lamb
  • 1 Tbls garlic paste
  • 1 Tbls ginger paste
  • 1 1/2 tsp hot chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 400ml water
  • 1 red bell pepper diced
  • 1 green bell pepper diced
  • 2 fresh green chillies sliced into disks
  • 450g fresh spinach roughly chopped
  • 3 Tbls fresh or frozen corriander chopped
  • 1 lemon rind & 1/2 the juice
  • 1 tsp extra garam masala

method

It's that time again where I say "brown the dreaded onions!" but that really is the only work involved here — bar a bit of chopping, combining & stirring and the rewards are huge. So get all your ingriedients ready in the order that they are listed & let's cook. Try to use a pan with a glass lid so you can keep an eye on your dish while it is covered.

Note: Adding lemon juice at the end of cooking retains the fresh citrus taste of the lemon, the earlier you add it, the more bitter it becomes, which is what you want in some dishes, but not this one!

  1. First of all, heat a heavy bottomed pan to hot.
  2. Add your oil & allow it to heat to a shimmer
  3. Add your onions & leave them alone, bar the odd check
  4. Combine all the spices & pastes including salt & pepper
  5. Stir fry until oil separates & the room smells amazing
  6. Check the dice of the meat is even

Once the onions are brown 20 mins of your life have passed yet again!

  1. Lower the heat to medium add your spice paste & stir fry
  2. Add your meat & stir fry
  3. Add 400ml water to cover the meat plus a little extra
  4. Bring it to the boil
  5. As soon as it boils — lower the heat & cover
  6. Allow to cook for 25 to 35 minutes

Do not remove the lid unless you feel it needs more water

  1. Add your peppers & stir fry 5mins to combine.
  2. When the peppers are nearly done — add your spinach
  3. Stir fry until the water from the spinach has disappeared — 7 to 10 mins.
  4. Add your green chillies & stir fry to combine

Your dish should have a very light sauce and be vibrant green. To keep it that way we are now going to add lemon rind & juice

  1. Add the lemon juice & rind
  2. stir to combine
  3. Add the extra garam masala
  4. Stir to combine
  5. Sprinkle with corriander & serve
brown suasages

Serve Lamb Curry with Spinach with plain boiled basmati rice, naan bread or, just with a parathas as it's a fairly dry curry making it the perfect finger food :¬P

Nutty Tip: Now! Frozen spinach can be used in this dish but, please try to use whole leaves & allow them to thaw and drain prior to adding to your dish. You will lose some colour using frozen but, when in a rush!
