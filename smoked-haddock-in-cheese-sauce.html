Smoked Haddock in Cheese Sauce

Smoked Haddock In Cheese Sauce is a rich dish full of butter & cheese. Do not be tempted to just use your strong cheddar, as it will be too salty and over power the fish, and, you will not get the colour we are after. The amount of fish is minimal making it a bit more affordable. A few more tricks to bulk it out means that 400g of frozen Smoked Haddock will serve 2 to 4. It is a very rich, filling dish, to which you could add spinich, broccoli or cauliflower to make it stretch to feeding four. But, it was just us two today, so we treated our selves to a pure indulgent fish bake!

  • 400g smoked haddock fillets
  • 1 small white onion finely chopped
  • 2 sticks celery finely chopped
  • 1 small leek finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp dyjon mustard
  • 1 veg stock cube crumbled
  • 1 pint milk
  • 120g salted butter*
  • 50g plain flour
  • 100g frozen peas
  • 200g red leicester
  • 50g strong white cheddar
  • Extra vegetables (optional)**

*Added in three stages

**If adding any extra vegetables be sure to par boil them for 5 minutes in salted water, if adding spinich; dry pan fry for 5 minutes to remove the moisture.

method

Now to begin with, I'm going to cook the chopped veg in the microwave as we want our sauce ingredients softened with no browning. However, you can use your stove top if you prefer, just take your time and soften the veg' in butter on a low heat. The fish will be partially baked, wrapped in baking paper and tin foil then baked in the oven then added to our sauce to finish cooking

  1. First of all pre-heat your oven to 180c
  2. Put 50g of butter into the bottom of an oven proof dish
  3. Add all your veg & microwave on high for 2 mins
  1. Stir & repeat for another two minutes
  1. Add all your seasoning & another 50g of butter
  2. Add the flour & milk, stir well
  3. Microwave for two for 2 mins
  4. Remove, stir, cook for another two minutes
  1. Add in your 200g of red leicester gently stir through
  1. Set the cheese sauce aside
  2. Create a parcel for your fish with 20g of butter
  1. Wrap it tightly & put in the oven for about 20 mins*

*The fish should be thawed and just starting to cook after 20 mins depending on the thickness of the fish.

  1. Take the fish out of the oven
  2. Remove the skin
  3. Break each fillet into large chunks
  4. Add the fish & juices to your sauce
  1. Top with your cheddar cheese
  1. Bake uncovered in the oven for 45 minutes @ 180c
