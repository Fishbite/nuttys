Pineapple pudding

Upside down!

Never fear making a pud. This one's dead easy, really quick to knock together, and just delicious served hot with custard, or cold out of the fridge, so I'm told by the fridge raiders! It's so incredibly juicy from the pineapple slices, perfect to finish many a meal off. I use a dark brown sugar for the cake mix, which gives it a lovely dark caramel taste; which is a little less sweet than white refined sugar.

serves 4 - 6

  • 75g dark brown sugar
  • 75g butter
  • 439g tin pineapple slices

    • save the juice for custard

    for the cake mix

  • 125g butter
  • 125g dark brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 Tblsp pineapple juice
  • A left over pineapple slice if there is one
  • 1 Tblsp baking powder
  • 125g flour

method

I use a food mixer for this recipe, but you can do it by hand, just make sure your butter is really soft. So, pop the oven on at 180c and grease an oven proof dish. By the time the oven's hot you'll be ready to rock!

Bye the way, you don't need to wash the mixer / mixing bowl out after making the topping, just scrape it clean with a spatular and you are ready to go with the cake mix ingredients

  1. Put the topping ingredients in a food mixer
  2. Set it to a medium speed
  3. Let the mixer run until butter and sugar are combined
  4. Grease an oven proof dish
  5. Spread topping into the base of the dish
  6. Place the pineapple slices on the topping
    7. layer the pineapple slices
  7. Put the first 5 cake ingredients into the food mixer
  8. Turn the mixer on to medium speed
  9. Allow the ingredients to combine
  10. Tip in the flour & allow to combine again

    11. NB: If mixing by hand, sift the flour & baking powder into a bowl through a sieve

  11. Pour the cake mixture onto the pineapple
    12. top with the cake mixture
  12. Bake in pre-heated oven @ 180c for 45mins
  13. Use a wooden skewer to check for doneness*

    14. *Prick the center of the cake with the skewer, it should come out dry (except the very tip which will have caramel on it) when the cake is cooked

  14. Allow to cool for a while so you can handle it
    15. allow to cool

    ***WARNING*** the topping is 180c caramel when it comes out of the oven, so, DO NOT attempt to turn it out until it has cooled to hand-hot

  15. Place a plate on top of the dish
  16. Say a little prayer!
  17. Flip the dish and plate over in one smooth movement
  18. Let the pudding drop onto the plate*

    19. *I bash it with a rolling pin! No patience!!!

  19. Carefully wriggle the dish off the pudding
    20. turn out onto a plate serve with custard with added pineapple juice
