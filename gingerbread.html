Sticky Gingerbread Cake

This will keep in a cake tin, or air-tight container, for a week. Try not to place it in a fridge as it'll spoil the texture of the cake. Easy and scrumy snack sized cakes that will be perfect after a huge sunday roast or popped onto a side plate with a lovely cup of tea.

serves 16 pieces

    Dry ingriedients

  • 300g plain Flour
  • 125g light brown sugar
  • 2 tsp ginger powder
  • 2 tsp freshly ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp Bicarb of soda or baking powder

    • Wet ingriedients

  • 2 beaten eggs
  • 175g black treacle
  • 175g golden syrup
  • 125g butter
  • 150ml cold water

    • to finish

  • 1/2 tsp icing sugar
  • jar of preserved ginger to decorate / top with

method

I'm using a food mixer to make this sticky ginger cake, but it can be easily be done by hand. You just need to add the wet ingredients gradually and finish with a whisk.

  1. Set the oven to 180c
  2. Grease and line a square tin with baking paper
  3. Put all the dry ingriedients into a food mixer

    4. or large mixing bowl and combine

  4. Put the wet ingriedients in a small pan
  5. Warm gently untill it's a liquid
  6. Add the cold water to cool it
  7. Put the eggs into the dry mixture
  8. Tip in the wet ingredients
  9. Blend the mixture until completely combined
  10. Pour the cake mix into the tin
    11. cake mix ready for oven
  11. put into the oven immediately for 40 to 45mins
  12. Check the center is cooked*

    13. *prick with a small skewer it should come out clean

  13. Transfere to a cooling rack
    14. allow to cool
  14. Once cool, cut into squares
  15. Dust with icing sugar
  16. Put a dab of preserved ginger on each square and serve
    17. allow to cool
    serve with a cup of tea

    Store in an air-tight container in a dark cupboard for up to one week
