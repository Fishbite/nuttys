Caribbean Creamy Pork Curry

Caribbean Creamy Pork Curry is completely different to most of my Caribbean dishes, not a fresh scocth bonnet in sight for starters. Greek yogurt & coconut cream create a delcate sauce. I always have a Caribbean spice mix in my cupboard it's so easy to make a spice paste with and the perfect blend of spices for this dish.

serves 4 - 6

ingredients to marinade

  • 350g pork leg steaks diced
  • 1 lime - juiced
  • 5 garlic cloves - crushed
  • 3cm fresh ginger - crushed
  • 3 Tbsp Dunns River Hot Caribbean curry powder
  • 1 tsp extra hot chilli powder (Optional)
  • 3 tablespoons greek yogurt
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil

ingredients curry

  • 3 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 4 med white onions - halved & sliced
  • 1 mixed pack of bell peppers sliced or diced
  • 1 tsp sea salt ground
  • 1 tsp black pepper ground
  • 450g greek yogurt
  • 65g creamed coconut cream
  • 1 450g tin of plum tomatoes crushed

method

Method for the marinade

  1. juice your lime into a pestle & mortar
  2. add your garlic cloves & ginger, bash to a paste
  3. add your curry powder & chilli powder if using
  4. add your vegetable oil & combine
  5. get a plastic pot with a lid & add your spice paste
  6. add yogurt & your diced pork
  7. seal the container & set into a fridge overnight

Method for the curry

Now this is an easy dish to cook once you have got your paste ready, just slice your onions while the pan heats & slice your peppers while the dish simmers. I cooked this in my dutch oven on the hob but any heavy based pan will be fine

  1. Heat a heavy based pan & add oil
  2. once hot add your onions to soften & brown just a little
  3. now add your marinated meat - have a lid to hand as it could spit at first
  4. remove the lid after it settles add salt & pepper brown your meat pieces & spices
  5. once browned add the rest of your yogurt & creamed coconut
  6. lower the heat cover with a tight fitting lid & cook for 30 minutes
  7. check if the meat is tender, if not continue to cook a for longer
  8. once tender add in your peppers & cook until peppers are softened, about 10 minutes uncovered
