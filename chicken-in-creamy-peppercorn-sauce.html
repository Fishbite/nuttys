Chicken in Creamy Peppercorn Sauce

Making my chicken in creamy peppercorn sauce is a similar process to my pork in creamy pepper sauce, but today I'm going to roast chicken legs in the oven and then use the sauce as a peppery gravy. Yummy! You can of course use diced meat and add it in to the sauce so it's a complete dish, or even utilise left over roast chicken. Whichever type of meat you choose to use you can be sure that chicken in creamy peppercorn sauce will be an absolute winner with its velvety decadence and peppery hit.

serves 4

for the sauce

  • 8 chestnut mushroom cut into slices
  • 4 streaky smoked bacon cut into 2cm pieces
  • 1/2 a medium onion finely diced
  • 1 stick of celery finely diced
  • 1 carrot grated
  • 3 cloves garlic, roughly chopped
  • 1 1/2 tsp black pepper corns roughly ground
  • 1 tsp salt
  • OXO™ veg stock cube crumbled
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 Tbls plain flour
  • 2 Tbls butter x 2
  • 3/4 pint of whole or semi skimmed milk
  • Left over gravy**

**Not essential, but do not use gravy granuals!!!

for the meat

  • 4 chicken fresh chicken legs*

*Alternatively, use fresh diced chicken or left over roast chicken. See the method for specific instructions for fresh diced chicken

method

To make my chicken in creamy peppercorn sauce I'm using fresh chicken legs that I'll roast in the oven, or air fryer actually, as it costs a little less to run than a conventional / fan oven. However, if you want to use fresh dice chicken, you need to add it to a hot wok, or heavy based pan and stir fry it for 10 minutes, then season it with salt and then set it aside while you make the sauce. Then you can add it to the sauce along with all the tasty juices that come out of the chicken while it has rested. Resting the chicken allows it to relax and soak up some of the juices so it becomes moist and succulently tender.

The following is the method to use fresh chicken legs with a note on the above if using fresh diced or left over chicken.

  1. Set your oven to 190c & allow it to get up to temperature*

*If using a conventional / fan oven

  1. Heat your wok, or heavy based pan to a med-high heat
  2. Add the bacon and let it brown
  3. Add the first lot of butter & allow it to melt
  4. Add all the vegetables, garlic, salt & pepper
  5. Stir fry for 10mins on a med-high heat
  6. Add mushrooms, stir fry for 5 mins
  7. Lower the heat to med, cover for 5 mins
  8. Push everything to the side of the pan
  9. Add the second lot of butter & allow it to melt
  10. Add the flour when the butter has melted
  11. Gently coax the flour into the butter to make a roux
  12. Slowly, add a little milk to the thick roux paste**

**Don't panic if it appears to go lumpy, just keep stirring, slowly adding the milk until it forms a smooth white sauce

  1. Add your left over gravy & Worcestershire sauce
  2. Stir to combine the ingredients
  3. Add your diced meat or left over chicken if using
  4. Lower the heat to low-med & cover for 10 mins***

***Increase the time to 30 mins if using fresh diced or left over chicken.

  1. Check the sauce half way through
  2. Add a little water if it seems too thick

When done, taste and adjust the seasoning by adding a little more salt & Worcestershire sauce if needed. It really depends on how salty the bacon is as to whether you need to do this.

serving suggestion

Creamy chicken and peppercorn sauce marries excellently with, plain boiled rice, mashed potatoes, new potatoes & veg if you wish, although veg are not an essential accomaniment as there are plenty hiding within the sauce!
