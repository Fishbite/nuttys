Balti Butter Chicken

Balti butter chicken now this is an amazing curry that many will know from Indian restaurants & take always. Buttery creamy delicately spiced and great for a dinner party or just a home away evening. I'm betting you assume you can not make it at home or that it's complicated. Well actually it is really easy to make. Yes there are a lotta ingredients might be a big knickers job. But basically once you have ground your spices and added them to yogurt & tinned tomatoes your ready to cook it is that simples! Now you can use Greek yogurt but definitely do not also add the cream! Unless you can not handle spice the cream & Greek yogurt will make a extremely mild curry. It's a fat ratio science thingy that is way too complicated for now.

Ingriedients for the marinade / sauce

  • 150ml Plain yogurt
  • 50g Ground almonds
  • 1 400g tin of plum tomatoes mashed
  • 1 tsp garlic paste
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 1 1/2 tsp hot chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp kashmiri chilli powder (optional)
  • 1 kg chicken skinned boned & cubed

Grind all the below to a powder if using whole & add to the marinade:

  • 1/4 tsp crushed bay leaves
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves (3 to 4 whole)
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon (1/2 inch whole)
  • 4 green cardamon pods
  • 1 1/4 tsp sea salt

Ingriedients to stir fry

  • 6 tbls ghee or unsalted butter (If using salted omit the salt!)
  • 1 tbls vegetable oil
  • 2 medium onions sliced
  • 4 tbls single cream (optional)
  • 2 to 4 fresh green chilli's finely sliced into disks (optional)
  • 2 tbls fresh or frozen coriander

method

Now I am using left over roast chicken for my Balti butter chicken as the mariande refreshes the meat. None of that dry left over chicken feeling here @ Nuttys kitchen. But there is really very little difference in the method using raw. Just be sure to check that the raw chicken is cooked through with a temperture guage or take a piece break it open if it white inside it's cooked. Another tip try to use thighs if your going to cook for longer as breast drys out much more easily.

Now I'm doing this today for a basic dinner. If I was doing it for a dinner party, well that's special & of course I would want to show off a bit more! I would want that authentic tandoor char on my chicken obviously it's a must. But as of yet I do not have a tandoor oven in my gadget range! Very annoying. So what you will need to do is after your meat's been marinated. Place it all into a colinder allow the majority of the mariade to drain off the chicken pieces. Spray the chicken with some of the melted oil ghee mixture of drizzle which evers easier. Now place the chicken under a very hot grill or into an air fryer turning once half way through. It will only need 2 to 4 minutes each side maximum depending on the size of the pieces. Place remaining oil & ghee into a hot pan once melted do your onions as above add your sauce and stir fry then add your grilled chicken & finish as above with chilli & corriander ;-) Simples....

Method for the marinade

  1. So firstly get all your ingriedients out check every thing is there then put all of the marinade into a sealable container
  2. Now grind your whole spices to a powder & add to your marinade pot
  3. Combine well add your chicken & set into a fridge over night or minimum 4 hours

Method for the curry

  1. Heat heavy based wok or pan once hot add your oil & ghee
  2. Once melted add your onions for about 5 minutes stir occasionally just softened down is perfect
  3. Add the chicken mixture & all the mariade / sauce
  4. Stir fry for 8 to 10 minutes untill the oil separates to the edges
  5. Add coriander chillies & cream if using and stir to combine soon as the cream's hot check your seasoning & serve

Balti butter chicken served with a plain basmati rice & popadoms or chapti breads
