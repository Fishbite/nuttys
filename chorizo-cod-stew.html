Chorizo Cod Stew

Baked Cod & Choritzo stew is a fantastic Spanish dish that is especially tasty, really easy to make and so different. In summer eat with shreaded lettice and which ever bread you choose or have. Winter time add creamy mashed potatoes or mashed roots. Season each layer of vegetables with a little salt & pepper as you add them to the pan, but then be sure to season your fish on both sides! I used a pointy sweet red pepper & cannellini beans but you could use butter beans if you prefer.

  • 400g frozen cod
  • 200g spicy choritzo sausage sliced
  • 1 onion diced
  • 2 sticks celary diced
  • 1 red pepper diced
  • 1 tbls fennel seeds
  • 1 tbls garlic paste
  • 400g 1 tin cannellini beans or butter beans
  • 400g 1 tin plum tomatoes mashed
  • 1 OXO™ veg stock cube crumbled
  • salt and pepper ground in stages
  • 2 tbls olive oil

method

As always chop & prepare everything. Leave your cod in the freezer until you're ready to set the dish into the oven.

  1. heat a heavy based pan add your choritzo crisp it up and let the oils release
  2. Pre-heat your oven to 180c
  3. once the pan glistens with oil and you have some crisping add onions
  4. stir through add celery
  5. stir through add peppers
  6. push everything aside in the pan add fennel seeds & garlic
  7. crumble your stock cube into your tinned tomatoes
  8. add your tinned tomatoes & veg stock cube & rinsed cannellini beans
  9. bring every thing to hot get a shallow oven dish & set the mixture in it
  10. get your frozen fish, season each side & push it down into the dish
  11. drizzle with olive oil
  12. bake for 25 to 35 minutes be guided by the pack of fish!

Please remember we are adding it to a hot stew base so the timings need to be varied slightly check the fish after 25 mins

Baked Cod & Choritzo stew. Served with creamy mashed potatoes or serve with finely sliced iceburg lettice & pitta bread
