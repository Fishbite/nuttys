Indian Sheperd's pie

If you love traditional Sheperd's Pie and also have a passion for a little spice this dish will be right up your street. The addition of fresh chilli's and some store cupboard spices will transform your comfort food into an Indian dinner. This is so easy to make, it may well become a goto version of classic with an Indian twist. It is healthier than my Lamb Mash Pie as it is topped with mashed potatoe & butternut squash made without butter or milk.

serves 4

For the Indian Sheperd's Pie mince

  • 2 Tbsp oil
  • 1 green chilli slit
  • 1 red chilli slit
  • 1 Tbsp ginger paste
  • 1 Tbsp garlic paste
  • 250g onions finely chopped
  • 2 carrots peeled & grated
  • 2 celery sticks finely diced
  • 1 tsp extra hot chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper powder
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp ground coriander powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin powder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala powder
  • 2 tsp tomato paste
  • 475 grams lamb mince
  • 2 Tbsp yogurt, whisked
  • A handful of chopped coriander
  • 100g frozen petite pois or garden peas

Optional Mushroom layer

  • 150g chestnut mushrooms
  • 2 tsp oil
  • 2 tsp butter
  • salt & pepper to taste

For the mash topping

  • 350g potatoes in skins
  • 450g Butternut squash peeled & diced
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 30g grated parmesan cheese
  • salt & white pepper to taste

method

This recipe is a four stage process:-

    • cook your mince
    • cook your optional layer of mushrooms
    • cook your potatoes & butternut squash*
    • mash both & assemble your pie

*Cook your potatoes & butternut squash in the microwave.

It really important to be organised with your ingredients — prepare well and you are bound to succeed! ;-).

I served this with a simple mint raita — which marries with the lamb and mash excellently :-P

Mince method

  1. Heat a heavy based pan & add the oil once hot then add the green & red chillies on a low heat till they look fried.
  2. Add the ginger and garlic paste and saute for a few seconds.
  3. Add the chopped onions and fry till they turn translucent.
  4. Add your carrots & celery fry till they have softened & reduced in volume.
  5. Add the tomato paste and fry for a few seconds.
  6. Reduce the heat completely and add the spice powders. Mix & fry for a few seconds before adding the minced meat.
  7. Add the meat on a medium high heat till sealed, around 3-4 minutes — continue to cook on medium heat (turning occassionally) till the meat is well browned..
  8. Then add the whisked yogurt, salt to taste and chopped coriander leaves.
  9. Add the frozen peas and continue to cook for another minute. Remove from the heat and keep aside.

mushroom layer method

  1. To make the optional mushroom layer, wipe then cut the mushrooms into thin slices.
  2. Heat a saucepan and add the butter & oil. Add the sliced mushrooms and fry them on a high heat till they soften and shrink in size.
  3. Season with salt & pepper. Remove from heat & keep aside.

Mash method

We are basically going to bake our potatoes in their skin, then remove the skins & discard. I vacuum packed my squash, pricked some holes in the bag & microwaved. You can simply cover with cling film if you do not have a vacuum pack machine and prick some holes in the film.

  1. Cook both your potatoes & squash separately in the microwave
  2. About 5 minutes per potato (until soft) as a rough guide
  3. About 5 minutes for the diced butternut squash — again this is a rough guide
  4. Once tender drain any liquid & mash them into a pan on a low heat.
  5. Add the egg yolk, salt, white pepper & grated parmesan cheese and mash everything together again.

Assemble the pie

  1. To assemble, grease a 9 x 9 inch square or 9 inch round ceramic or glass, oven safe dish with butter.
  2. Add the cooked mince mixture and level off the surface.
  3. Top it up with the optional mushroom layer or directly add the mashed potato & butternut squash mixture
  4. Level off the surface of the mashed potatoes & butternut squash mixture, then run a fork through the surface to form a pretty design.
  5. Place the dish into the air fryer @ 180c for 15 to 20 minutes if using a conventional oven see method below
  6. Preheat an oven at 180 degrees C and bake for 12 minutes.
  7. Then change the oven setting to 'grill' mode and grill for 3-4 minutes or till the pie gets a beautiful golden crust.
  8. Remove, cool slightly and then gently run a knife through to create 4 equal portions.
  9. Serve hot with a cooling mint raita
