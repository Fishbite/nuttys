ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

serves 4

  • 2 x sprays oil
  • 1 kg Beef Brisket Joint
  • 4 medium (300g) carrots cut into chucks
  • 1 large (150g) parsnip cut into chucks
  • 3 sticks (100) celery cut into chucks
  • 1 large (200g) white onion cut into chucks
  • 1 1/2 tsp Sea salt roughly ground
  • 2 tsp Black peppercorn roughly ground
  • 2 beef OXO™ cubes crumbled
  • 1 veg OXO™ cube crumbled
  • 2 tsp English mustard powder
  • 3 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp Worcester sauce or Hendersons™ relish
  • 750 ml Boiling water
  • 1/2 Tbsp oil or butter
  • 1/2 Tbsp plain flour
  • 1 Tbsp bisto powder*

*enough cold water to form a slurry

method

I am using an electric slow cooker for my Slow Cooked Beef Brisket but if you do not have one of those this recipe is probably not for you :-) Sorry just kidding you can achieve the dish with a pressure cooker far quicker but for timing that be quided by your brand. Now this is really fast to put together & really, really, no really slow to cook 10 to 12 hours and an hour resting required for the meat. I am not even going to peel the carrots something rarely stated in Nuttys kitchen! No fine dicing or exact sizes just large dice for all the vegetables so it is gonna be a breeze.

The most important task is sealing & browning our meat in a really hot pan I have my stir-fry ring on max to achieve that quickly. So lets get all our vegetables prepped & our seasoning out, weighed, ground and do this ting.

So excited about tomorrows dinner mashed potatoes & sweetheart cabbage all prepared.....12 ish hrs of smelling that pot is either going to have me salavating or drive me completely Nuts:-(

(Edit from IT "To late Nutty, that days long past!")

Now, to make things really easy, prep' everything needed, then life will be a dream and you can put this together, leave it and forget it. Happy days :-)

  1. So fistly place your carrot chucks into the bottom of your slow cooker.
    • 4 medium (300g) carrots cut into chucks
    • 1 large (150g) parsnip cut into chucks
    • 3 sticks (100) celery cut into chucks
    • 1 large (200g) white onion cut into chucks
  2. Add in your parsnips chunks on top & then your celery chunks lastly add onions
  1. Place 750 ml of water into the kettle ready to boil. Check your seasoning are all ready to go.
  2. Now remove packaging from the Beef Brisket pat the joint dry with kitchen paper.
  3. Place a heavy based frying pan on your fastest ring set to max and once smoking spray twice with spray oil.
  4. Find the place on the joint with the most fat and set in down into the pan try not to move it till you can hear it browned.
  1. Then turn and continue section by section until you have achieved a deap crisp brown all arround the Joint then brown both flat ends.
  1. Turn the ring off and place the Joint on top of all your vegetables.
  1. Top the joint and vegetables with Salt & papper we prepared earlier
    • 1½ tsp Sea salt roughly ground
    • 2 tsp Black peppercorn roughly ground
  1. Boil the kettel add 750 ml of the boiling water into your meat frying pan to deglaze it
  1. Add all your seasonings:
    • 2 beef OXO™ cubes crumbled
    • 1 veg OXO™ cube crumbled
    • 2 tsp English mustard powder
    • 3 tsp smoked paprika
  1. Add Worcester sauce or Hendersons™ relish
  1. Stir until the powdered stock cube has disolved
  1. Carefully pour the liquid around the side of the meat leaving the meat untouched.
  1. Set on high for 1 hour then reduce to low for 10hrs*.

*Please do not be tempted to 'Peek' everytime you open the lid your adding 30 minutes to the cooking time, as you have released the heat.

10hrs later.....So our beef should be melt in the mouth done.

  1. Test the joint is tender, return to the pot & cook some more if not.
  1. Carefully remove the joint to a plate to rest for 1 hour.
  1. Now we need to thicken the gravy so get your remaining ingredients.
    • 1/2 Tbsp oil or butter
    • 1/2 Tbsp plain flour
    • 1 Tbsp bisto powder*
  2. Get a small pan and melt the oil or butter add in the flour and stir to cook out the flour.
  1. Put Bisto™ into a small pot / cup and add a little water to make a surry.
  2. Add Bisto™ slurry to the slow cooker
  1. Stir through and turn the slow cooker to high to thicken up as the meat continues to rest.

Now I start boiling my potatoes for my mash, praying that it was all worth the wait??? = The answer is it was Nutty to ADD REASON

Carve the joint, thick or thin — we tried it both ways, and we prefer thin. It doesn't matter either way, beacuse even if you manage to cut slices, they are going to fall apart when you try to pick them up! Promise!!!

We like to return the sliced beef to the gravy and veg — which is actuallly the tastiest of stews that you can possibly make.

Just taste this!
