Pear & Almond Tart

A no-flour pudding, simply devine! When I saw this recipe I thought 'how can that possibly go wrong?' Well, cooking anything takes practice to perfect and this is no exception! It's a really simple recipe, but it must have a hot stone, like a pizza stone, to sit on in the oven. This is my first attempt and thus, it looks a little rustic round the edges! Yep! I thought I knew better and omitted the stone as I dind't have one to hand!!! It made me smile when it came out of the oven and grin from ear to ear when I tasted it. ...mmmm yummy!

ingredients

This is what you need :¬)

serves 4

  • 100g butter
  • 100g ground almonds
  • 1 tsp cornflour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 med egg
  • 1 tin pear halves in juice
  • Save pear juice for custard!
  • Icing sugar to dust with

method

No flour required! For best results, this heavenly desert needs to be cooked on a hot pizza stone, or similar. If you don't have a pizza stone, never fear! You can always use an earthenware dish turned upside down and pre-heated in the oven. A food mixer is also quite handy to cream the cake mix, which contains almonds. I'm using ground almonds, but you could use flaked and grind them yourself if that's what you have to hand

  1. Put the stone or substitute in the oven
  2. Set temp to 180c
  3. Tip the first 5 ingredients into a food mixer
  4. Set speed to med
  5. Let the ingredients form a thick creamy paste
    6. cream the cake mix
  6. Meanwhile, line a cake tin with baking paper
    7. line the cake tin
  7. Drain the pear halves & save the juice
    8. drain the pears halves
  8. Spread the cake mix into the lined tin
    9. spread mix into the tin
  9. Lay the pears on top of the cake mix
  10. Leave some room between the pears
    11. place pears on top
  11. Put the cake tin on top of the stone in the oven
  12. Bake for 20 to 25mins @ 180C
  13. Take the tart out of the oven
  14. Remove cake tin & allow to cool
    15. allow to cool
  15. Place onto a serving plate
    16. ready to eat!

    Still practicing this one, it was nearly a disaster, but I just about rescued it! It really does need that hot stone or earthenware to sit on in the oven, otherwise you get a soggy bottom. ...And nobody wants a soggy bottom do they!!!

    I hope you enjoy this devilishly delightful desert, it will be excellent served warm with soft creamy vanilla ice cream ;¬>)
