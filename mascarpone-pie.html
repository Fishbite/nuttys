Mascarpone Pie

I invented this dish in a purely Nutty moment when I decided to make cheesecake from memory... Turns out my memory can fail me as I bought two pots of Mascarpone Cheese some double cream and no actual cream cheese — so this revelation came to life. ;-) And oh I "thank the lord" it did — such a delicate flavour and so indulgently creamy with gorgeous biscuity scrumminess. No baking required here — simplicity can be delicious — just top with fresh fruit, or as I have done here a black cherry pie filling.

Click the Method tab below to see a step-by-step guide on how to make Mascarpone Pie along with some pictures as a guide.

ingredients

Here's what you need :¬)

serves 8

  • 250g digestive biscuits
  • 100g butter
  • 100g icing sugar
  • 600g mascarpone cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  • 280ml thick double cream
  • *410g Black cherry topping

* the topping is optional as a fruit compote or fresh fruit would also be perfect or simply serve as it is naked.

method

You will need a small pan to heat your butter for the base and a pre-lined 23cm loose bottomed cake tin. A blender is handy to crush the biscuits but not essential as this can easily be done with a bag & a rolling pin especially if you need to vent some frustration.

  1. Heat a small saucepan with 100g of butter till just melted & add your 250g crushed biscuits combine well
  2. Press the biscuit mix into a lined tin & chill
  3. Sieve the 100g icing sugar into a large bowl add 600g mascarpone cream & mix well
  4. Add 1 tsp vanilla essence & the 280ml double cream & combine well
  5. Once your biscuit base has completely cooled you can now top with your cream mixture smoothing the top with a pallet knife
  6. make a lip around the edge to hold the topping
  1. Place into the fridge until ready to serve

I have topped mine with a tin of Princess Fillings & Toppings — Black Cherry which is a fast and easy pie topping. But you could make a fruit compote or simply top with fresh berries of your choosing.
